About

Digital Accessibility advocate and former Mobility Coordinator for the New York City Department of Transportation.

Hilda Diana's interest in digital accessibility began while working for the City, where she experienced first-hand the barriers that prevent a vast segment of the population, including Black, Indigenous, Persons of Color (BIPOC) with disabilities from fully participating in decision-making that affects their livelihood; whether that is navigating public spaces or accessing digital information. Through software engineering, Diana seeks to incorporate the experiences and needs of underserved populations to contribute toward achieving disability and social justice in public spaces and the digital world.

In her free time Hilda Diana finds joy in cooking, and recovering and reupholstering furniture!
